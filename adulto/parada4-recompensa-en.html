The Gothic Legacy

The Gothic Legacy: The Gothic Quarter is the legacy itself. During this era, Barcelona became a Mediterranean power thanks to its guilds and its Consulate of the Sea. The great noble families built the palaces that still define the area's landscape today.

Curiosity of the Place: The famous Bishop's Bridge, despite its appearance, is not Gothic. It's a neo-Gothic creation from 1928! The architect, Joan Rubió i Bellver, included a skull pierced by a dagger on the underside, a supposed curse on the city if it is ever removed.

Source: Dietari de la ciutat f. 123r
