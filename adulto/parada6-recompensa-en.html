The Archdeacon's Palace

Living History: You are facing the renovation that Lluís Desplà carried out in the 16th century, turning a medieval house into a Renaissance palace. But its story doesn't end there: today, this building guards the written memory of all Barcelonans, as it is the headquarters of the Historical Archive of the City of Barcelona.

An Ironic Wink: The mailbox you used for the enigma is Modernist (1902). The swallows symbolize the ideal swiftness of Justice, but the turtle represents the sad reality: the administrative bureaucracy that makes it slow and heavy.

Source: City Historical Archive

JUMP CLUE:
Your excavation advances towards the Age of Enlightenment. Look for a street with the name of a "watchman" (Veguer) just behind the great King's Square. There, the inventory numbers await you.

Direct Clue
